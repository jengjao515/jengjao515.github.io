Arrays and Hashes lists and more date posted 04/03/2015 21:24

Alright, so what is the difference between Arrays and Hashes? That is this week's question. Well, let's start off with defining them first.

In Ruby, an Array is a list that can be called on later by their place in the list. It looks like this in code:

ArrayName =
["your friend", "my name", 2, "322", 12038]

A Hash is also a list, however, everything in the list is paired with something else. It looks like this in code.

HashName = {
:my_dog => "Freddy",
:your_dog => "Mitchell"
:breed1 => "Golden Retriever"
:breed2 => "Chihuahua" }

So now you've seen what they look like, how does Ruby use them? I'll compare an array to your typical guest list. Let's say you're planning a small party and you need to refer to the list. You would say "so and so is first on the list". In Ruby, you would refer to the first person as guestList[0]. For Ruby, the first person or thing in an array has position [0]. (So the second person would be [1], third person [2], etc.)

A hash is similar to your phonebook. Every name on your list refers to something else on the list. Maybe you forgot your friend's phone number and his name is Mike. You'd simply go your contacts list and search his name which would at some point lead to you dialing his number. A hash works similarly. The words on the left side of the list are referred to as 'keys' which are connected to 'values' on the right. So in this case, Mike would be a key, and his phone number would be a value. When you would call any of these keys/values in Ruby, you would type in phonebook["Mike"], and his phone number would show as a result.

Still a little confused? Maybe it'll help to think of it like this. Searching for "Mike" who is INSIDE your phonebook is the KEY to finding how much you VALUE his phonenumber.

See what I did there?

I hope my explanations made sense to you and especially you sir/mam, the non-tech person. Thanks for reading!
