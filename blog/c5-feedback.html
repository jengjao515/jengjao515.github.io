Don't like group work? Too bad. A day of pairing and feedback date posted 04/17/2015 19:13

At DBC, you're required to do at least 3 pairing sessions a week and the purpose is so that you can learn from and get to know other students. I absolutely hated any kind of group work in college and it's not that I minded the teamwork aspect, the problem was if there would actually BE any kind of teamwork. DBC approaches teamwork and pairing issues differently, they allow us to give anonymous feedback to each other, which is then looked over by DBC, and rated by other DBC students.

My first pairing was completely nervewracking. I had no idea what to expect, despite reading over the challenge we'd be going over. Although, I had just recently graduated, I was never good at properly communicating in group projects. Working alone was something I was good at because I'm horrible at explaining myself. It was definitely rewarding when my partner was understanding but I knew that eventually my anxiety would come into play with someone less patient. During the more successful pairings, my partner and I were able to work together through research and just enough communication. Probably the most frustrating thing so far is that there is a lot of discrepancy between students who've been studying several programming languages beforehand and those who are just starting. I like to participate in group work so since I'm pretty new to everything, it's frustrating when I can't properly participate and slow my other partner down. Again, anxiety comes into play.

Before I even read my first few pieces of feedback, I already knew what most of them were going to say. I'm pretty self-aware, so when I saw that most of them mentioned how quiet I was, well, I didn't really know what to do about it. I thought that maybe if I studied harder, got the concepts better, this problem would just gradually go away with the confidence that comes with knowledge. But I am just naturally quiet, if I start talking, sometimes I over compensate. I want to say that I hope to develop finding a middle somehow, but I've struggled with this my whole life. DBC wants us to create this awareness in our partner about how we're feeling before we get right to work. Sometimes I think about telling my partner, "Hey, so I'm just a really quiet person that isn't actually comfortable with speaking." I like letting my work speak for itself, but in the end, I know this isn't how the real world works.

Communication is key in pretty much every aspect of life. So I definitely want to figure out this 'quiet' problem but I'm stuck. When I write feedback for other people, I feel bad because I think maybe it would've helped both of our performances if I communicated better. So when I pair with someone who's pretty patient, I always give better feedback because I'm less aware of how quiet I am, I actually feel comfortable. As a result, I write better feedback for this person which isn't fair for those that struggle with my quiet persona.

I feel that DBC is using feedback to create less hostile work environments. They want future programmers to not just be efficient in their work, but also, likeable. All this pairing and feedback makes me think about how I could apply it for future work environments. It's also motivating because I'm not just learning how to program, but also how to be a people person.

My only critique is that I actually handle criticism pretty well. I really do take it in and think about it, so if I get feedback that's obviously holding back, I feel like I haven't really gotten anything from it. So if you pair with me and you don't like something, you can even tell me during the pairing, I won't be offended. If it's the truth, at least, I can do something about it and it'll be better for the both of us.
